Kimchi (김치)

What is Kimchi?

Kimchi is a very popular and traditional Korean dish made with various fermented vegetables. Kimchi has several variations making it very versitile.

Kimchi is a staple food in Korean cuisine, and most Koreans eat Kimchi with at least one meal in the day. It is usually made with cabbage, raddish, and cucumber.

Ingredients

  • One medium(~2 lbs) head napa cabbage
  • One-fourth cup salt
  • Water
  • One tbsp (5-6 cloves) grated garlic
  • One tsp grated peeled fresh ginger
  • One tsp sugar
  • Two tbsp salted shrimp paste
  • One to five tbsp Korean red pepper flakes
  • Eight ounces Korean radish, peeled and cut into matchsticks
  • Four medium scallions, trimmed and cut into one inch long pieces

Equipment

  • Something to weigh the kimchi down, such as a filled can of beans
  • One quart jar with lid
  • Bowl/plate to place under jar

Instructions

  1. Cut the cabbage through the stem into quarters, cut cores from each piece. Cut each quarter into 2-inch-wide strips.
  2. Place cabbage into large bowl and sprinkle with salt. Massage cabbage until soft.
  3. Add water to bowl until water is covering the cabbage. Put plate on top of cabbage and weigh it down for 1-2 hours.
  4. Rinse and drain the cabbage under cold water 3 times. Set aside to drain for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Add garlic, ginger, sugar, and shrimp paste into a bowl and stir until a paste. Stir in the pepper flakes.
  6. Carefully squeeze any water out of the cabbage and add it into the paste. Add raddish and scallions.
  7. Thoroughly mix using hands.
  8. Put mixture into a jar. Pack it down until the brine rises to cover the vegetabkes.
  9. Place bowl/plate under jar to catch any overflow. Let the jar ferment at cool room temperature, out of sunlight, for 1-5 days.
  10. Check on the kimchi every day, opening jar and pressing down to keep it submerged. When it tastes to your liking, transfer jar to fridge.
